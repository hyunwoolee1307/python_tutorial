Title: Interactions Among the Western North Pacific Subtropical High, Indian Summer Monsoon, and East Asia Summer Monsoon: Implications for East Asia Summer Monsoon

Abstract
The East Asian summer monsoon (EASM) system is modulated by large-scale circulation features that originate in the Indian and western North Pacific basins, notably the Western North Pacific Subtropical High (WNPSH) and the interface between the Indian summer monsoon (ISM) and EASM. This synthesis integrates evidence from multiple studies to elucidate (i) how WNPSH variability links the strength and regional rainfall distribution of the EASM, (ii) how the Indian monsoon–East Asian monsoon interaction modulates the WNPSH and moisture transport toward East Asia, and (iii) how interannual to decadal variability in the WNPSH and ISM–EASM coupling affects East Asia summer rainfall patterns. We emphasize mechanisms (Rossby wave trains, moisture advection, jet and anticyclone adjustments, and teleconnections) supported by diverse observational and modeling studies.

  1. The WNPSH as a central modulator of East Asia summer rainfall and the EASM
    • The Western North Pacific Subtropical High (WNPSH) exerts strong control on East Asian summer monsoon circulation through its intensity, position, and associated moisture fluxes. Seasonal and interannual adjustments of the WNPSH are linked to the vigor and geographic extent of the EASM rainfall belt and the Meiyu/Baiu front, mediated by the subtropical high’s interaction with moisture pathways from the tropics to East Asia \citep{10.1029/2019gl084705}, \citep{10.1175/2011jcli4057.1}. The WNPSH’s northward shift and strengthening are associated with enhanced southerlies and increased convection over subtropical East Asia, contributing to a more vigorous EASM, whereas a weakened or displaced WNPSH corresponds to reduced EASM rainfall in key regions \citep{10.1029/2022jd038399}, \citep{10.1029/2022jd037371}. This robust linkage is reinforced by modeling and observational syntheses showing that regional errors in East Asian rainfall and circulation often arise from biases or misrepresentation of the WNPSH dynamics in the tropics and maritime continents \citep{10.5194/wcd-5-711-2024}.

  • Mechanistically, WNPSH variability modulates the monsoon trough, moisture transport, and regional convergence. For example, a northward or eastward shift of the WNPSH is associated with enhanced moisture convergence over East Asia and a strengthened EASM flow, while a southward/westward shift reduces terrestrial convection over the East Asia–Western North Pacific domain \citep{10.1029/2022jd038399}, \citep{10.1175/2011jcli4057.1}. These patterns are consistent with the “subtropical high–tropics moisture flux” framework, wherein the high acts as a conduit for lower- and middle-tropospheric moisture transported from the tropical oceans toward East Asia during boreal summer \citep{10.15666/aeer/1605_71557170}.

  • Evidence from multi-model and observational analyses indicates that inter-model spread in EASM rainfall is linked to how CMIP6 models simulate WNPSH dynamics and related circulations. Positive and negative modes of inter-model variability align with enhanced or reduced WNPSH intensity and a coherent reorganization of jet streams and atmospheric circulations that ultimately governs East Asian rainfall distribution \citep{10.1029/2022jd037371}. In addition, the WNPSH’s interaction with subtropical jets can shape the intraseasonal evolution of the East Asian monsoon rainfall bands, including the summer rainband and attendant monsoon surges \citep{10.3389/feart.2020.00234}.

  1. Indian summer monsoon–East Asian summer monsoon coupling and its influence on the WNPSH and moisture transport
    • The interface between the Indian summer monsoon (ISM) and East Asian summer monsoon (EASM) is a crucial determinant of regional hydroclimate in East Asia. An index capturing this interface reveals two teleconnection patterns that regulate the ISM–EASM interaction and its impact on droughts and floods in East Asia. Positive coupling is associated with anomalies that strengthen the ISM–EASM linkage and modulate moisture transport pathways toward East Asia, including the WNPSH’s behavior, while disruptions to this coupling can weaken EASM moisture transport and alter precipitation patterns \citep{10.1029/2012jd017841}.

  • Interdecadal variability in the ISM–EASM interface modulates the WNPSH through its modulation of the regional pressure fields and jet configurations that steer moisture and convective activity. For instance, the APO-like (Anticyclone–Pacific Oscillation-like) and NAT-like (North Atlantic–Tropical) patterns described in the literature contribute to an eastward shift of the interface and an accompanying amplification of WNPSH, which enhances southwesterly flows toward East Asia and intensifies the EASM during positive phases. Conversely, opposing patterns can reduce WNPSH influence and suppress EASM vigor \citep{10.1029/2022jd038399}.

  • The Indian Ocean–Pacific teleconnections and the Indo-Pacific convection oscillation (IPCO) further elucidate how Indo-Pacific convective anomalies during boreal summer feed back onto the WNPSH and East Asian rainfall. Negative IPCO phases, characterized by anomalously enhanced convection over the North Indian Ocean and suppressed convection over the western North Pacific, tend to favor a weakened EASM with increased Yangtze River valley rainfall, driven by the complex interplay among Ural blocking, the Okhotsk high, and the subtropical western Pacific high (WNPSH) \citep{10.1002/asl2.418}.

  • The ISM–EASM interface interacts with the South Asian westerlies and the Moho (Meiyu) front’s evolution, thereby linking large-scale ISM variability to East Asian precipitation via WNPSH and related subtropical circulation changes. This coupling is consistent with the observed sensitivity of the East Asian rainfall belt to ISM variability and the role of the Indian Ocean–western Pacific circulation in shaping the EASM regime \citep{10.1029/2012jd017841}.

  1. Mechanistic pathways linking WNPSH, ISM/EASM coupling, and East Asia summer monsoon rainfall
    • Rossby wave trains and isentropic uplift from the Tibetan Plateau serve as a central mechanism by which ISM perturbations and Tibetan Plateau heating influence the WNPSH and moisture convergence in East Asia. Tibetan Plateau warming can modify two distinct Rossby wave trains, deform the western Pacific subtropical high, and enhance moisture convergence toward the East Asia subtropical front, linking TP temperature changes to changes in East Asian summer rainfall and WNPSH position \citep{10.1029/2008gl034330}. This mechanism supports a cohesive picture in which Tibetan Plateau–ISM–EASM interactions modulate WNPSH and East Asian rainfall via large-scale wave dynamics.

  • Regional relaxation experiments (nudging) in initialized hindcasts demonstrate that errors in the Maritime Continent region propagate to East Asia summer monsoon circulation through the WNPSH. This study highlights the sensitivity of EASM forecasts to regional forcings and the central role of WNPSH in mediating error growth and predictability, reinforcing the WNPSH as a pivotal node linking ISM variability and East Asian rainfall \citep{10.5194/wcd-5-711-2024}.

  • Quasi-biweekly and intraseasonal oscillations (QBWO and ISO) over the western North Pacific and adjacent basins modulate the EASM by perturbing the WNPSH and moisture delivery to East Asia. The QBWO-driven downstream wave trains alter the SCS monsoon trough, EASM flow, and associated upper-level jets, contributing to variability in East Asian precipitation on weekly-to-biweekly timescales that feed into the seasonal mean EASM pattern. These dynamics illustrate how shorter-timescale variability (QBWO/ISO) interacts with the longer-timescale WNPSH–EASM relationship \citep{10.1002/jgrd.50422}, \citep{10.3390/atmos13081222}.

  • The interplay between ISM and East Asian monsoon systems is further clarified by indices describing their joint state. Comparative studies show that improved representation of the interface between ISM and EASM improves the interpretation of regional droughts and floods, in part through better capturing the WNPSH configuration and moisture transport pathways \citep{10.1029/2012jd017841}.

  1. Implications for East Asia summer monsoon rainfall predictability and regional hydroclimate
    • The WNPSH–EASM relationship supports enhanced predictability of East Asia summer monsoon rainfall when reliable representations of WNPSH variability are achieved. A faithful WNPSH representation correlates with EASM strength and regional rainfall, suggesting that improved WNPSH predictability can enhance East Asian rainfall forecasts and better anticipate Meiyu/Baiu flooding or drought events \citep{10.1073/pnas.1214626110}.

  • Inter-model spread in CMIP6 simulations of EASM rainfall is linked to how models reproduce the WNPSH and its associated teleconnections. Credible forecasts require accurate simulation of the WNPSH–EASM linkage and the ISM–EASM interface, including the downstream effects on moisture fluxes and upper-tropospheric circulation \citep{10.1029/2022jd037371}.

  • Proxy and paleoclimate perspectives corroborate modern relationships by showing persistent WNPSH sensitivity to basin-scale forcings and their influence on East Asian rainfall across different climates and timescales. Studies on boreal summer insolation effects imply that WNPSH dynamics—and therefore EASM—are sensitive to regional heating patterns, teleconnections, and basin-scale monsoon interactions \citep{10.5194/cp-20-2017-2024}, \citep{10.3354/cr01019}.

Conclusion
A coherent synthesis of the literature indicates that the Western North Pacific Subtropical High is a central conduit through which Indian summer monsoon variability and regional teleconnections modulate the East Asian summer monsoon. The ISM–EASM coupling, modulated by Tibetan Plateau warming, Rossby wave dynamics, and regional moisture transport, shapes the WNPSH’s intensity and position, thereby controlling East Asia summer rainfall distribution. Short-timescale oscillations (QBWO, ISO) act as amplifiers or modulators of this system, influencing the seasonal evolution and intraseasonal variability of EASM. Efficient prediction and understanding of East Asia summer rainfall thus require integrated representations of WNPSH dynamics, ISM–EASM coupling, and their coupled wave-transport pathways, as supported by the cross-cutting evidence from observational analyses, regional experiments, and climate model simulations cited herein \citep{10.1073/pnas.1214626110}, \citep{10.1029/2008gl034330}, \citep{10.5194/wcd-5-711-2024}, \citep{10.1029/2022jd038399}, \citep{10.1029/2012jd017841}, \citep{10.1029/2022jd037371}, \citep{10.1002/jgrd.50422}, \citep{10.1002/asl2.418}, \citep{10.3390/atmos13081222}, \citep{10.1029/2006gl027474}, \citep{10.1175/2011jcli4057.1}.
